Common Causes of Ear Pain:

  • Ear Infections (Otitis Media/Externa): Bacterial or viral infections are a leading cause of ear pain, especially in children.
  • Wax Build-up: Excessive earwax can block the ear canal, causing pain and temporary hearing loss.
  • Sinus Infections: Sinusitis can lead to referred pain in the ears.
  • Throat Infections: Sore throat or tonsillitis may radiate pain to the ears.
  • Foreign Objects: Inserting objects into the ear, common in kids, can cause injury and pain.
  • Barotrauma: Pressure changes during flights or swimming can result in ear discomfort.

Symptoms to Watch For:

  • Sharp, dull, or throbbing ear pain
  • Hearing loss or muffled hearing
  • Fluid discharge from the ear
  • Fever, irritability (especially in children)
  • Fullness or pressure in the ear
  • Difficulty sleeping due to pain

When to See an ENT Specialist?

If your ear pain is severe, lasts more than a day, or is accompanied by hearing loss, fever, or discharge, consult an ENT doctor immediately. Early diagnosis and treatment can prevent complications and restore ear health quickly.
